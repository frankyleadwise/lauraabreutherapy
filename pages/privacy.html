Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters deeply. This page explains how Laura Abreu Therapy collects, uses, and protects your information.

Last updated: January 1, 2025

1. Overview

Laura Abreu Therapy ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and the confidentiality of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information when you visit our website (lauraabreutherapy.com) or contact us about our services.

This policy applies to information collected through this website only. It does not apply to your protected health information (PHI) collected during the course of clinical services, which is governed by our separate Notice of Privacy Practices provided to clients at intake, in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

2. Information We Collect

Information you provide voluntarily: When you contact us via our website contact form, you may provide your name, email address, phone number, and a description of what brings you to therapy. This information is used solely to respond to your inquiry.

Automatically collected information: Like most websites, we may automatically collect certain technical information when you visit our site, including:

  • Your IP address and general geographic region
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on the site
  • Referring URLs

This information is collected in aggregate and is used to understand how visitors interact with our website in order to improve it.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide to:

  • Respond to your inquiry or consultation request
  • Provide information about our services
  • Schedule appointments via our secure client portal
  • Improve our website and the information we provide

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Protected Health Information (PHI)

Any health information shared during clinical services is considered Protected Health Information (PHI) under HIPAA. Clinical communication occurs through our HIPAA-compliant client portal — not through this website or unencrypted email.

Please do not send sensitive health information via the contact form on this website. If you are an existing client with a clinical matter, please contact us through the secure client portal.

As a licensed mental health professional, Laura Abreu is required to maintain the confidentiality of your PHI. Detailed information about how your clinical information is handled is provided in the Notice of Privacy Practices given to all clients at the start of treatment.

5. Confidentiality and Mandatory Reporting

All information shared in therapy is confidential. There are limited exceptions required by law, including:

  • Imminent risk of harm to yourself or others
  • Mandatory reporting of suspected child abuse or neglect
  • Mandatory reporting of abuse of vulnerable adults
  • Court orders or valid legal process

These exceptions will be explained in full during your intake session.

6. Cookies and Tracking

Our website may use cookies — small text files stored on your device — to understand how visitors use our site and to improve the user experience. You can disable cookies through your browser settings, though this may affect some site functionality.

We do not use third-party advertising cookies or tracking pixels for advertising retargeting.

7. Third-Party Services

Our website may link to third-party services including our client booking portal (SimplePractice / ClientSecure), which has its own privacy policy and security practices. We encourage you to review the privacy practices of any third-party service before providing them with personal information.

If you subscribe to our email newsletter, your email address will be stored with our email service provider. You may unsubscribe at any time.

8. Data Security

We take reasonable precautions to protect your personal information. Our clinical client portal is HIPAA-compliant and uses encryption to protect sensitive data. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Children's Privacy

Our website and services are not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us immediately and we will take steps to delete it.

10. Your Rights

Depending on your state of residence, you may have rights regarding your personal information, including the right to access, correct, or request deletion of information we hold about you. To exercise these rights, please contact us at laura.abreu.therapy@gmail.com.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page reflects when the policy was last revised. Continued use of this website after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
